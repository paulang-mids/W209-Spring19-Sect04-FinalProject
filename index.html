US Pollutants & Health Risk Analysis

Making Insights Accessible

Introduction

In 2010, 1 in 35 deaths in the U.S. were attributed to illness caused by air pollution. According to the study from which the statistic originated the total number of air pollution related deaths in 2010 was half of those in 1990. Since 1990, air pollution has been identified by government agencies like the CDC and EPA as a real health issue to be understood and addressed. According to the National Emissions Inventory Report (NEI), the Environmental Protection Agency compiles air emissions across the United States every three years. The data collected has, in the last decade, been made available to civilians in publicly hosted access tables and a summary report (NEI), however the mode of dessimination lacks ease of use for the average individual.

About

Goal

Create a publicly accessible interactive map that provides a simple and effective mode for individuals to look up location specific air pollution information and its associated risk level. Increase overall awareness of air pollution outside of mainstream perception of carbon emission as the only threat to US air quality and population health.
